Our Mission

Living your full potential in an ever-changing world. We are committed to equipping women and girls with the tools, opportunities, and support needed to overcome barriers, adapt to change, and lead with confidence. Through education, economic empowerment, and community-driven initiatives, we inspire women to unlock their potential and create lasting impact in their families and societies.

Our Work

We provide vital grants to high-impact, vetted partners implementing economic empowerment programs for women experiencing poverty, locally and around the world.

Our Vision

Improved livelihoods for women and inclusion in decision making. We envision a world where every woman has the opportunity to thrive economically, participate fully in community leadership, and shape the decisions that impact her life. By fostering equality, access, and empowerment, we aim to build resilient families and inclusive societies led by strong, informed women.

OUR STORY

COVID-19 had a severe economic impact in Africa, with Kenya among the hardest hit. In urban areas like Nairobi, the lockdown devastated informal sector jobs, forcing many to return to rural homes where life is more affordable.

Women faced the greatest burden—caring for families, seeking manual work, and supporting children out of school. Teenage girls were especially vulnerable, with a sharp rise in unintended pregnancies and disrupted education.

Limited access to clean water, lack of accurate health information, and widespread misinformation worsened the situation. These challenges led to the creation of the Women Empowerment Program in 2019 to support and uplift affected women and girls.

What we have been doing at DiD (Doing It Different)

Many women are the breadwinners across most homes in urban and rural areas, and they have been the hardest hit by the pandemic as they work in the informal sector. Doing It Different has been identifying households and mobilizing dry foodstuff for the poorest families headed by women (widows, single parents, and the separated).

We are now piloting support for these women (those whose small businesses have collapsed or are on the verge of collapsing) to set up and restart as the economy slowly opens up. We are also supporting new ventures with soft loans of $60, with a grace period of six months—after which they can begin repaying (at no interest), so the funds can help the next woman in need.
